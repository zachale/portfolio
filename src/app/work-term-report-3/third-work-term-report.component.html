Summer 2025 @ ApplyBoard
  I spent my third work term as a software engineering intern at ApplyBoard. This was an exciting opportunity to work with a new company and work in an office for the first time in the industry. I developed backend and DevOps solutions.
  ApplyBoard builds a platform that simplifies the study abroad search, application, and acceptance process by connecting international students, recruitment partners, and academic institutions on one platform. Their customers include students from around the world and educational institutions. I worked with a subsidiary of ApplyBoard called Capio. Capio's primary focus is on helping schools manage international student applications.
The Capio team including Zach
I contributed to Capio's serverless backend services throughout my internship. As a software engineering intern, I was tasked with designing and implementing new services and improving existing services based on user stories. The user stories were typically defined by the product team, my manager, or my co-interns. To implement these changes and updates, I primarily used TypeScript, AWS SDK, and AWS CDK. I delivered robust, impactful services and updates that handled thousands of requests and interacted with thousands of AWS Lambda functions.
One of the key projects I worked on during my third term was modernizing a legacy export service. The legacy service was not scalable, had high maintenance costs, and was a bespoke solution for a single client. My team and I were tasked with replacing this system with a more scalable and maintainable solution. I was given the opportunity to contribute to the effort from system design through implementation. It was a valuable learning experience to work in a high-agency environment and collaborate with the team to deliver the system to specification. I worked closely with three other interns and regularly sought guidance from senior engineers when needed. During development, our team encountered a challenging decision point where we were unsure about our implementation approach. Through team discussion and collaboration, I was able to find a solution that worked well for everyone involved. This experience helped me develop my leadership and collaboration skills, which were noted positively in my work term evaluation. When all was said and done, I delivered a system that replaced the legacy service and saved ApplyBoard thousands of dollars in annual cloud costs.
My summer term at ApplyBoard was another incredible experience. The technical and professional skills I developed will prove invaluable as I continue my career in software engineering. I look forward to what the future has in store, and I am excited to apply my new skills in my next co-op term.

Learning Goals
Leadership
I have always found myself leaning towards leadership roles in team settings, and this term I wanted to make a direct effort to improve these skills. Working on a team with three other interns presented an opportunity to develop these abilities while collaborating on group deliverables. Early in my term, a technical leader encouraged me to stay vocal and continue leading by example. The challenge was learning to balance taking initiative with fostering team collaboration. When our team reached a point of decision paralysis in our implementation approach, I helped us move forward by making decisions, prioritizing tasks, and delegating responsibilities. I also served as Scrum Master for four weeks and facilitated four retrospectives, which developed my facilitation skills. This experience, along with positive feedback in my work term evaluation from my manager, has helped me grow my leadership skills immensely.
Planning and Organization
A piece of feedback I received from my previous term was that I needed to focus on improving my time management and planning skills. This was a major focus of my third term, and actually it became a major challenge as well. Being on an intern only team and working fully autonomously made it difficult to be able to accurately create estimates for my work. Without direct supervision, I was at the mercy of any unknown issues that could arise in the codebases that I had never seen. There were many moments when me or my team became blocked because of unforeseen issues within unfamiliar codebases that we were working in. While I completed more than 15 tickets meeting acceptance criteria and quality expectations, there were times where I was unable to hit my deadlines due. However, I did receive overwhelmingly positive feedback from senior engineers, including recognition of my AWS knowledge and work ethic, demonstrating that I delivered high-quality features. While this was challenging, it led to me further developing my planning skills as a whole. Creating accurate timeline estimates and executing on them will continue to be a focus for me as I progress in my career.
Problem Solving
System design became a primary arena for sharpening my problem solving this term. I took leadership of designing and implementing a stateless service for client data export, led four architectural discussions, and produced infrastructure diagrams and comprehensive documentation. This work provided hands-on system design experience and showed that I can contribute meaningfully to medium-scale architecture within my work stream. Before this term, I had never had the oppourtunity to work with AWS, or design a fully cloud based solution. This experience has given me the confidence to tackle more complex technical problems and has helped me grow as a software engineer.
