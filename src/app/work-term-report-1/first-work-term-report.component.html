Summer 2024 @ 10KC
  I spent my first work term as a software engineering intern at 10KC Technologies. While working with 10KC, I was an independent contributor as part of their engineering team, developing full-stack solutions to resolve client feedback, meet new feature requests, and fix critical bugs.
  10KC builds a SAAS web application that focuses on democratizing opportunity and empowering social learning within organizations. Their customers include the likes of RBC, BMO, and Thomas Reuters. Their application has proven results in increasing equity within organizations and facilitating learning amongst peers and mentors through 10KC’s various solutions.
I was contributing to 10KC's web application for the duration of my internship. As a software engineering intern, I was tasked with building and maintaining full-stack features based on specifications and fixing critical bugs. These specifications could arise from direct customer or user feedback, from a UI/UX designer, or a myriad of other stakeholders. To build these features I utilized the MEAN stack which includes technologies like Typescript, Angular, Express.Js, Node and MongoDB. Jest was also used to build robust unit tests for all new functionality I developed. I also got exposure to non-code technologies as well. I built a handful of data funnels, allowing me to measure the usability and effectiveness of my Features. I also helped localize the application, using internal tools to build out features in 5 different languages. Overall, I built robust, impactful features and solutions that accrued thousands of user impressions, and fulfilled specifications.
My experience at 10KC was certainly unforgettable. The technical and professional skills I learned have opened up new opportunities for me and have introduced me to the industry of software engineering. I look forward to applying these skills moving forward and continuing to grow as a developer.
Goals
Cross-team collaboration
Improving my cross-team collaboration was a very important goal for me this work term. I had never experienced working in a dynamic environment with people of different talents, so diving in and embracing this environment was key. I achieved this goal by consistently working with product, engineering, and customer-facing teams to collaborate, design, and deploy large projects. This has strengthened my ability to collaborate and has allowed me to share my opinion in a room of different talents and experiences.
Engineering and problem solving
Because this was my first Co-op experience, I knew it would be critical for me to focus on building strong fundamentals in problem-solving, and engineering. One way I achieved this goal was by reviewing a large amount of code. I participated in a number of large and small code reviews, on varying parts of the 10KC stack, exposing myself to new web development techniques, nuances, and solutions. I was also given the privilege to take part in 2 solution development sessions where I contributed my ideas and brainstormed with coworkers. Solving problems collaboratively in this way opened me up to unique viewpoints I would have never considered otherwise. Seeking out and participating in these reviews and events boosted my skillset immensely, solidifying my skills in problem-solving and engineering.
Software development
Over this first work term, I also wanted to focus on building my general software development skills. I accomplished this goal much more than I anticipated. I achieved 300+ GitHub contributions and 30+ closed tickets. On top of this, I got to apply myself in so many different technical scenarios. I was able to hotfix 4 critical bugs that had reached production which directly stabilized the product. I also got to handle driving a handful of full releases, leading them from testing to production. Additionally, I built and took ownership of 2 full larger scale features as well as several smaller fixes and features. In general, I was consistently placed in a position of ownership and development work.
